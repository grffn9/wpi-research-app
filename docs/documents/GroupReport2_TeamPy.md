## 1. How you used Generative AI

Describe specific tasks where you used AI tools (e.g., code generation, debugging, polishing UI, testing).
* If debugging became exceptionally strenuous and time-consuming, generative AI proved quite helpful in pointing us in the right direction.
* Generative AI was also useful for generating HTML that was highly structured and boilerplate, which reduced production time.
* Additionally, there were some cases where forgetting the correct syntax made a tool like GitHub Copilot very useful, as you know what you need to do and can just describe it to the GenAI.

Which tasks did it help you the most?
* Boilerplate code generation (specifically HTML)
* Reference for syntax/functions
* Debugging

## 2. Impact on your development process

How did AI tools influence your productivity and collaboration?
* We believe that GenAI influenced productivity primarily by reducing the time spent researching syntax and cutting out some of the copy-and-paste boilerplate code generation that would otherwise take quite a bit of time. Overall, it did enhance productivity, however, by allowing more time to think about **how** something is implemented, as opposed to **what** the implementation would be.
* In terms of collaboration, we saw no influence here.

Did AI change how your team divided responsibilities?
* No, we divided responsibility in a very conventional way, taking a similar number of user stories or attempting to match the quantity of work based on intuition. Either way, GenAI was not a consideration here.

Did AI save time, create additional work, or shape design decisions?
* AI was primarily responsible for saving time, allowing us to focus on what we thought were the more major components of the project. We believe there was no negative impact from it creating additional work, and neither did it shape our design decisions in any way.

## 3. Evaluation of benefits and drawbacks

What were the most useful aspects of using AI?
* We found that productivity enhancements were the primary benefit of AI. Its capabilities surrounding general knowledge made it an effective teammate.

What challenges, inaccuracies, or limitations did you encounter?
* As long as we made our prompts thorough and context-filled, the AI delivered with complete accuracy. If we didn't provide enough context, the models would not provide an answer adequate for what we were looking for, but this is a very solvable problem.

Did you need to correct or verify AI-generated content?
* Everything was verified through manual and, eventually, automated testing, so yes, we did. There was nothing on the corrections side, however.

## 4. Your overall perspective

Do you feel the use of AI tools enhanced or hindered your learning?
* Enhanced. We believe that it gave us more freedom to focus on the aspects of software engineering that are difficult: the **how** of feature implementation and application design. AI removed the need for some of the monotony of application development, allowing us to focus on the more critical parts of software engineering.

What guidelines would you recommend for future students using AI in software engineering projects?
* Be specific and context-rich in your prompts. Contrary to our intuition with using Google search, the more input and context you can provide to the model, the better a response you will get. It is similar to the common phrase, "garbage in, garbage out." If you don't provide a good, in-depth prompt, you will get garbage.
